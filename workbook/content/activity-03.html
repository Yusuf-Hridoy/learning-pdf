In the last activity, you put your morning routine in order. But here's the cool part — you do that SAME routine almost every single day. That's called a loop — when something repeats over and over!

Loop

Loop — Something that repeats. Instead of writing the same step 100 times, you just say "do this 100 times."
Example: When you do 10 jumping jacks, that's a loop! You're repeating the same move 10 times instead of writing "jump and clap, jump and clap, jump and clap..." over and over.

Basic Version

Find the Repeat — Read each one. Circle the part that repeats, then fill in the blanks below.

1. Song: "If You're Happy and You Know It"
If you're happy and you know it, clap your hands (clap clap)
If you're happy and you know it, clap your hands (clap clap)
If you're happy and you know it, then your face will surely show it
If you're happy and you know it, clap your hands (clap clap)
What repeats? How many times?
2. Workout: P.E. Warm-Up
Do 10 jumping jacks.
Rest for 5 seconds.
Do 10 jumping jacks.
Rest for 5 seconds.
Do 10 jumping jacks.
What repeats? How many times?
3. Recipe: Cooking Pancakes
Pour batter onto the hot pan.
Wait until you see bubbles.
Flip the pancake.
Cook 1 more minute.
Take it out — then start the next one!
Hint: how many pancakes if you make 5?
What repeats? How many times?
4. Daily Habit: Brushing Your Teeth
Every morning, brush your teeth.
Every night, brush your teeth.
Do this every single day for a whole week.
What repeats? How many times in a week?

Teacher / Parent Note

This activity introduces loops (also called iteration) — one of the three core control structures in programming, alongside sequence (Activity 2) and selection (coming up in Activity 5). Kids learn that repetition is everywhere in real life, and that programmers use loops to avoid writing the same code over and over — which saves time AND prevents mistakes. Discussion prompt: Ask your child to spot 3 loops in their own daily life — at home, at school, or in a hobby.
