What is an Algorithm?

Ages 8-10
MASCOT
GOES
HERE

An algorithm is just a list of steps to do something. Even making a sandwich is an algorithm! Let's see if you can write one.

Algorithm

Algorithm — A step-by-step set of instructions to solve a problem or complete a task.

Basic Version

Write 5 steps to make a peanut butter sandwich. Be specific!

Challenge Version

Write your steps, then have someone follow them exactly. Did it work? What was missing?

Reflection Box

Self-Check

  • I used at least 5 steps
  • My steps are in the right order
  • I was specific (e.g., "open the bag" not just "get bread")
  • I tested my algorithm on a friend or family member

Bonus: Cut & Sort!

Cut out these steps and glue them in the correct order.

Cut along the dashed lines
Spread peanut butter Open the bread bag Take two slices of bread Close the sandwich Get a butter knife
Teacher / Parent Note

This activity introduces the core CS concept that algorithms are precise instructions. Common gap kids miss: opening the bread bag, using a knife, etc. — the lesson is that computers need extreme specificity.
