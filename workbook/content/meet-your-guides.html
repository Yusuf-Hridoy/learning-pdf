Meet Your Guides

Throughout this workbook, three helpful characters will join you on your journey. They'll introduce new concepts, help you think through tricky problems, and keep you company along the way!

CHARACTER
ILLUSTRATION

PIXEL

Curious, brave, learning right alongside you

Pixel is a curious kid who loves asking "why?" and "what if?" — the two most powerful questions in computer science. Pixel appears at the start of most activities to introduce new concepts and to help you get excited about what you're about to learn.

CHARACTER
ILLUSTRATION

BYTE

Careful, thoughtful, great at spotting bugs

Byte is Pixel's best friend — a little more cautious, always double-checking the work. Byte is the workbook's debugger, asking questions like "Did you try it?" or "What if that's wrong?" When you're stuck on a hard activity, imagine Byte looking over your shoulder — that voice IS how real programmers think.

CHARACTER
ILLUSTRATION

BOLT

Literal, obedient, does EXACTLY what you tell it

Bolt is a small robot who joins you in Activity 11 (Treasure Hunt Grid). Bolt only understands 3 commands: MOVE FORWARD, TURN LEFT, TURN RIGHT — nothing else. That means Bolt will do exactly what you say, which is either amazing (when your instructions are right) or a total disaster (when they're wrong). Bolt is a great teacher because Bolt has NO common sense — just like real computers!

Look for Pixel and Byte in the corner of each activity — they're your learning companions. And don't forget: YOU are the algorithm designer here. The guides are along for the ride!
