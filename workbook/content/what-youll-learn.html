What You'll Learn

This workbook builds computational thinking skills step by step. Each activity teaches ONE new concept, then combines it with what came before — so by Activity 11, you're using everything together, and by Activity 12, YOU are the designer.

The 12 Activities
1

What is an Algorithm?

Learn what makes a set of instructions an algorithm.

2

Sequencing Puzzle

Discover why the ORDER of steps matters.

3

Spot the Loop

Recognize repetition in songs, chores, and patterns.

4

Write Your Own Loop

Use "REPEAT N TIMES" to shorten programs.

5

Yes/No Conditionals

Make programs decide with IF / THEN / OTHERWISE.

6

Nested Conditionals

Chain multiple Yes/No questions into decision trees.

7

Debug the Robot

Find bugs — sequence, missing-step, and wrong-value.

8

Bubble Sort

Learn your first NAMED algorithm.

9

Selection Sort

Discover that the same problem has multiple solutions.

10

Pattern Recognition

Find the RULE that predicts the future.

11

Treasure Hunt Grid

Combine everything to navigate a puzzle.

12

Design Your Own Algorithm

YOU'RE the designer. Build from scratch.

Skills You'll Build
Computational thinking—breaking problems into parts and building solutions
Logical reasoning—following rules to reach conclusions
Problem decomposition—turning big challenges into smaller steps
Pattern recognition—spotting rules and predicting outcomes
Debugging skills—finding and fixing mistakes
Creative problem-solving—designing your own solutions
