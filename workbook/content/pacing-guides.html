Pacing Guides

Choose the schedule that fits your teaching or learning rhythm. These pacing plans expand on the 3 pacing options introduced in the How to Use page — pick the one that fits and adapt as needed.

1-Week Intensive Plan

For summer camps, spring break, or focused CS immersion. 2–3 activities per day for 5–7 days.

DAY 1 — Foundations of Algorithms
  • Activity 1: What is an Algorithm?
  • Activity 2: Sequencing Puzzle
  • Discussion: What's the difference between random steps and an algorithm?
DAY 2 — Loops
  • Activity 3: Spot the Loop
  • Activity 4: Write Your Own Loop
  • Hands On: Do the Loop Bonus outside (pattern hunt)
DAY 3 — Conditionals
  • Activity 5: Yes/No Conditionals
  • Activity 6: Nested Conditionals
  • Discussion: Draw a decision tree for your morning routine
DAY 4 — Debugging & Sorting Part 1
  • Activity 7: Debug the Robot
  • Activity 8: Bubble Sort
  • Hands On: Do the "Sort Real Things" bonus with household objects
DAY 5 — Sorting Part 2 & Patterns
  • Activity 9: Selection Sort
  • Activity 10: Pattern Recognition
  • Reflection: How is selection sort different from bubble sort?
DAY 6 — Integration
  • Activity 11: Treasure Hunt Grid
  • Note: Take a break — this one is meaty!
DAY 7 — Capstone
  • Activity 12: Design Your Own Algorithm
  • Complete the Vocabulary Review, Mini-Quiz, and Reflection Page
  • Celebration: Celebration! Kids get to teach someone one concept

4-Week Unit Plan

For homeschool, after-school programs, or supplementary CS. 3 activities per week for 4 weeks.

WEEK 1 — Foundations of Algorithms
  • Activity 1: What is an Algorithm?
  • Activity 2: Sequencing Puzzle
  • Activity 3: Spot the Loop
  • Reflection: Weekly reflection: Where did you see loops today?
WEEK 2 — Loops & Conditionals
  • Activity 4: Write Your Own Loop
  • Activity 5: Yes/No Conditionals
  • Activity 6: Nested Conditionals
  • Discussion: Design a decision tree for a weekly household task
WEEK 3 — Debugging & Sorting
  • Activity 7: Debug the Robot
  • Activity 8: Bubble Sort
  • Activity 9: Selection Sort
  • Hands On: Do the "Algorithm Race" bonus with a family member
WEEK 4 — Integration & Capstone
  • Activity 10: Pattern Recognition
  • Activity 11: Treasure Hunt Grid
  • Activity 12: Design Your Own Algorithm
  • Complete Vocabulary Review, Mini-Quiz, and Reflection Page

12-Week Semester Plan

For elementary CS class or gentle weekly rhythm. 1 activity per week for 12 weeks.

WeekActivityFocus
1 Activity 1 — What is an Algorithm? Definitions
2 Activity 2 — Sequencing Puzzle Order matters
3 Activity 3 — Spot the Loop Recognizing repetition
4 Activity 4 — Write Your Own Loop Writing loops
5 Activity 5 — Yes/No Conditionals Decision-making
6 Activity 6 — Nested Conditionals Decision trees
7 Activity 7 — Debug the Robot Finding bugs
8 Activity 8 — Bubble Sort First named algorithm
9 Activity 9 — Selection Sort Comparing algorithms
10 Activity 10 — Pattern Recognition Finding rules
11 Activity 11 — Treasure Hunt Grid Integration
12 Activity 12 — Design Your Own Algorithm Synthesis

Reminder: Every activity has both a Basic (ages 8-10) and Challenge (ages 10-12) version. Kids can revisit any activity in Challenge mode after completing Basic — that's the built-in extension.
